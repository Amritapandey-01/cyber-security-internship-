About ISO 27001

Information Security Management System

ISO/IEC 27001 is published jointly by the International Organization for Standardization (ISO) and the International Electrotechnical Commission (IEC). The current edition, ISO/IEC 27001:2022, replaced the 2013 version and restructured Annex A from 14 domains into 4 themes containing 93 controls. It uses the Plan-Do-Check-Act (PDCA) cycle to help organizations of any size or sector identify information security risks and apply proportionate controls to treat them.

Certification is issued by an accredited certification body (e.g. one accredited by UKAS, ANAB, or another national accreditation body) after a two-stage audit — a documentation review (Stage 1) followed by an on-site implementation audit (Stage 2). Certificates are valid for three years, with annual surveillance audits and a recertification audit before expiry. This portal summarizes the management clauses, Annex A control themes, auditor checklist items, and a typical implementation timeline.

ISO/IEC 27002:2022 Annex A Controls

Annex A of ISO/IEC 27001:2022 lists 93 controls grouped into 4 themes. Organizations select applicable controls based on their risk assessment and record the justification in a Statement of Applicability (SoA).

🏢 Organizational Controls (37)

Policies for information security, roles & responsibilities, threat intelligence, supplier relationships, incident management, business continuity, legal & contractual requirements, and independent reviews of the ISMS.

👤 People Controls (8)

Screening, terms & conditions of employment, security awareness training, disciplinary processes, remote working, and confidentiality or non-disclosure agreements.

🏭 Physical Controls (14)

Physical security perimeters, secure offices and facilities, equipment maintenance, clear desk & clear screen policy, secure disposal of media, and protection against environmental threats.

💻 Technological Controls (34)

Identity & access management, authentication (including MFA), cryptography, network security, secure configuration, malware protection, logging & monitoring, data leakage prevention, and secure development lifecycle.

🆔 Identity Management

Every user should have a unique identity mapped to a real person. Shared or generic accounts must be approved, restricted, and monitored, and unused accounts should be disabled promptly (control A.5.16).

🔑 Authentication (A.5.17, A.8.5)

Secure authentication information management, password complexity and rotation policy where used, multi-factor authentication for privileged and remote access, and encrypted transmission of credentials.

🔐 Access Control (A.5.15, A.5.18, A.8.2–A.8.3)

Apply least-privilege and need-to-know, review access rights at planned intervals, restrict privileged access rights, and enforce formal access provisioning and de-provisioning.

📊 Risk Assessment (Clause 6.1.2)

Identify information security risks, analyze likelihood and impact against defined criteria, evaluate against risk acceptance criteria, and produce a Risk Treatment Plan (RTP) linked to the SoA.

ISO 27001 Auditor Checklist

✔ Clause 4 - Context

Determine internal & external issues, stakeholders, scope of ISMS, business objectives, and documented processes.

✔ Clause 5 - Leadership

Verify leadership commitment, security policy, roles, responsibilities, resource allocation, and communication.

✔ Clause 6 - Planning

Risk assessment, risk treatment, security objectives, Statement of Applicability (SoA), and planning for changes.

✔ Clause 7 - Support

Resources, competence, training, awareness, communication, documentation, and document control.

✔ Clause 8 - Operation

Operational planning, risk assessments, implementation, risk treatment, security controls, and monitoring.

✔ Clause 9 - Performance Evaluation

Internal audits, monitoring, measurement, KPIs, management reviews, and continual evaluation.

✔ Clause 10 - Improvement

Corrective actions, non-conformities, root cause analysis, continuous ISMS improvements, and lessons learned.

✔ Evidence Collection

Policies, risk registers, training records, meeting minutes, audit reports, asset inventory, and SoA documents.
